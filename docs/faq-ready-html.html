Questions farmers usually ask

Short answers. If something's still unclear, write to us and we'll tell you straight.

How much time per week does this take?
About 2 to 3 hours a week. Some of that is short videos and community time. A good chunk of it is doing the thing on your own farm, so it counts as real work, not extra homework on top of your day.

Is the program in English?
Yes, English only. All the content, the coaching sessions, and the community run in English.

What if I join in the middle of a cohort?
You can join any time without feeling lost. The content for the current monthly goal explains what it is, why it matters, and how to tackle it, so you can jump in from there. Coaching sessions and the community forum are there for your questions.

What happens right after I sign up?
You'll get a short welcome email right away, and a few more over the next couple of weeks that give you context on why we built this, how the greenhouse piece fits, and what the program looks like in practice. You can read them when you have time.

Can I leave anytime?
Yes. It's month to month, so you can drop any time if it's not bringing you value. You can also miss a week and pick it up the next one, no penalty.

Do I need to grow tomatoes already, or am I planning to?
The program is built for farmers already growing greenhouse indeterminate tomatoes, because everything centers on improving that crop in the greenhouse you already have. If you're planning to grow them but haven't yet, this is probably a stretch. Better to wait until you have a season under your belt.

What makes this different from a course?
A course hands you information. This is a group of farmers working on the same monthly goal at the same time, with live coaching when you get stuck. The videos are short, five minutes, and the playbook grows from what farmers surface in their own fields. Less watching, more doing, and you're not alone with it.
