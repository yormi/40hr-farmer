# Dan Quotes (Broadfork Farm)

Source: presentation on automation and yield, Dan from Broadfork Farm (Richmond, VA). Two-acre intensive operation, four 30x96 high tunnels, year-round production.

Quotes below are verbatim from the transcript Guillaume pasted. Tagged by the three themes we're mining for the landing page.

---

## Working less / automation as pressure relief

**The strongest candidate for the `#lever` section:**

> "What I realized with automation was that we could get better quality and better yield in the wintertime with the tunnel being totally automated without us there than if we were there managing the tunnels without automation. Literally, the tunnel will do a better job by itself."

Why it lands: it's the most direct articulation of "automation beats constant attendance." It also reframes the 24/7 greenhouse problem from "I have to be there" to "I don't have to be there, and it does better without me." Pairs perfectly with the `#lever` paragraph on cutting half the beds or automating the work.

---

> "We have four children, and automation helps us continue to run the farm when we're not there."

Why it lands: human, specific, not abstract. Ties "working less" directly to life (kids, being present). This is the one to use if the callout is family-framed rather than operations-framed.

---

> "The last thing was quality of life improved because the greenhouse controller and motors are doing it for us."

Why it lands: names the feeling ("quality of life") without being preachy. Quieter tone.

---

> "When we go out with a hand crank, we crank it up and walk away; the tunnel is doing it totally differently and much more precisely."

Why it lands: concrete before/after image. Less quotable as a standalone callout, stronger inside a paragraph.

---

## Reducing losses

> "In the wintertime, as we were controlling ventilation with humidity sensors, we started to see disease go down, which meant yield went up dramatically."

Why it lands: causal and specific. Disease goes down → yield goes up. Useful for the `#translation` section when we talk about what climate control actually buys you.

---

## Improving yields

> "Once we got our tunnels automated, that's when we started to see improvement in yield and quality."

---

> "Every time we automate even one thing on the tunnel, we get good results in multiple ways."

Why it lands: frames automation as compounding, not a big-bang investment. Each small move pays.

---

> "We wish we had done it sooner. If I were to go back in time, I'd do this five years ago. We would have seen better yields earlier."

Why it lands: regret-framed, farmer-to-farmer. Strong for a "why wait" moment.
