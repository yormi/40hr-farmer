# Upstream Promotion Plan, 40hr Farmer

Internal, team-facing. Drafted 2026-04-17. Window: 8 weeks to cohort 1 (May/June 2026).

## 1. Objective

Fill cohort 1 with 50 market gardeners who arrive on the landing page already warm. Upstream channels build the context (firefighting recognition, greenhouse economics, Orisha/Drew trust) so the landing page activates instead of creating it.

Every asset must be reusable inside the landing page, welcome sequence, or sales call. No single-use content.

### Audience math

| Channel | Reach |
|---|---|
| GFM magazine subscribers | 5,000 |
| GFM Instagram | 50,000 |
| GFM email list | 24,000 |
| Orisha user list | 4,000 |
| **Total (nominal, with overlap)** | **~83,000** |

50 seats sit comfortably inside the pool. The real constraint is Guillaume's bandwidth to support 50 farmers well, not acquisition volume. This is a careful activation of warm audiences, not an urgent push.

## 2. Anchor framing

Headline framing (Guillaume endorsed, use verbatim): **"It's not a biological limit. The conditions we offer the plant are."** Humble, honest, strategic. This line anchors the GFM piece and seeds every other asset.

## 3. Channels

### 3.1 GFM (highest leverage)

Three sub-channels, all owned by Andrew, Guillaume reviews voice, Antoine polishes copy.

- **Magazine feature article:** 1,500 to 2,500 words on greenhouse tomato economics. Program as closing note, not ad. [TBD: confirm editorial deadline with Andrew.]
- **Dedicated email send to 24k list:** confirmed feasible with Andrew. Single highest-leverage conversion event of the 8 weeks. Book the slot early.
- **Instagram collab, 50k audience:** 2 to 3 posts (carousel plus reel), timed with the article and dedicated send.

Needed: outline approved by Guillaume, 2 to 3 photos from `assets/clients/ferme-decembre/`, numbers cleared (10x greenhouse vs field bed, 5x top grower vs average, Drew's ~4x in 2 years as personal anchor). Sources: Orisha data, best-in-class Quebec yields, USDA averages, conference talks. Industrial greenhouse 10x is a ceiling reference, not a target.

Measurement: UTM-tagged links, split GFM sub-channel attribution in GA4 and HubSpot.

### 3.2 Drew, Ghost House Farm

Peer trust. Drew's own story (roughly 4x yield in 2 years) is the best real-world proof for the biological-limit framing.

- **Shoot:** next week (Apr 20 to Apr 26). Andrew runs it remotely. Drew self-shoots b-roll from Andrew's guidance. Backup if Drew slips: Scott (another Orisha user).
- **Outputs:** one long-form video (15 to 25 min) on Drew's channel, 3 to 5 short-form clips, one written Q&A hosted on the landing page (doubles as case study).

Needed: 2-hour recording block locked, interview guide (Antoine drafts, Guillaume reviews), shooting basics for Drew, release permission, UTM.

### 3.3 Guillaume's socials

Direct line to warm network. Guillaume talks like Guillaume, not a brand account.

- 2 to 3 posts per week (LinkedIn plus whatever else). Mix: scene, belief, soft cohort mention.
- 1 slightly longer post per week (150 to 300 words) repurposable as an Orisha user email.
- Target: 20 min per week of Guillaume input. Antoine edits for voice, LB schedules.

### 3.4 Long-form owned content

- **Drew case study** (week 3 or 4): narrative from the shoot.
- **Day in the life of a 40hr farm** (week 2): written piece plus 8 to 12 photos, real Tuesday.
- **FAQ deep dive** (week 5 or 6): honest objections, plain language.
- **Sample-week preview (parked):** Month 1 challenge only. Blocked on Guillaume defining that challenge. Not on the 8-week calendar.

Antoine drafts, Guillaume reviews, LB handles imagery. Andrew contributes on strategic/greenhouse pieces.

### 3.5 Orisha user list (4k)

Already trust us. They need an invitation and a reason to forward.

- Launch email from Guillaume, week 2 or 3.
- Follow-up "cohort fills soon" email, week 6 or 7. No fake scarcity.
- Needed: clean segment in HubSpot excluding waitlist; short shareable blurb.

## 6. Risks and flags

- **Guillaume's time is the real bottleneck, not reach.** Plan assumes ~20 min/week socials input and ~2 hr/week reviews. If bandwidth slips, slow activation first, do not cut cohort support quality.
- **Drew availability.** Scott is the confirmed backup.
- **Remote shoot quality.** Andrew approves a short test clip before full capture.
- **GFM calendar.** Print piece may trail into cohort 2. The dedicated 24k send lands inside the window regardless, and is the highest-leverage asset. [TBD: confirm editorial deadline with Andrew.]
- **GFM dedicated send slot.** Lock early. If week 6 slips, fall back to week 7 or 8.
- **Sample-week preview.** Parked until Guillaume defines Month 1 challenge.
- **Voice drift.** Antoine owns final voice pass (no dashes, farmer-is-hero) on anything Guillaume does not personally write.
- **Cohort pacing.** If week 5 submits trend below 25, rework copy/creative rather than ramping volume. If volume overshoots 50 with quality, waitlist cohort 2 rather than expanding cohort 1.

## 7. Cohort 2+ (brief)

Evergreen assets compound. Capture 3 to 5 short video testimonials in the last 2 weeks of cohort 1. Sample-week preview ships once Month 1 challenge is defined, becomes cohort 2 lead magnet. Podcast and partnership outreach deferred from cohort 1 to protect focus, revisit once cohort 1 is full and post-mortem is complete.

Upstream is a habit, not a launch campaign. The 8-week plan is the first rep.
