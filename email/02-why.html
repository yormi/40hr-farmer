A few years ago, we started noticing a pattern.

We'd talk to growers who were doing everything right — great crops, loyal customers, solid markets. But when you asked how they were doing, really doing, the answer was always some version of the same thing: exhausted.

70-hour weeks. No days off in season. Partners who barely saw them. The farm was working, but the life around it wasn't.

The thing is, most of them knew something needed to change. But where do you start? The knowledge to actually cut labor without cutting sales is scattered — bits in research papers, bits in the heads of a few experts, bits you only learn by getting it wrong.

That's what this project is about. Getting that knowledge out of people's heads and into a playbook that any grower can use.

More on what that actually looks like in a few days.
