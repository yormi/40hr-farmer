Here's something we learned the hard way: the biggest labor savings on a diversified farm are hiding in the greenhouse.

Most field beds bring in low hundreds of dollars. A well-run greenhouse bed can bring in thousands. And greenhouse crops are already where you spend most of your time.

So if you can increase your yields per bed — really increase them — you can grow fewer beds, hit the same sales targets, and get hours back every week.

The best growers get 5x more tomatoes than the average. That's not genetics or luck. It's technique — pollination timing, temperature management, plant vigor. Stuff that's highly technical but very learnable.

That's where we start. One crop at a time. Tomatoes first.

The math is simple: double your yields, cut half the beds. Each bed you cut removes walking, weeding, troubleshooting — the hidden labor that eats your week.
