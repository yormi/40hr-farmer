The 40hr Farmer

Quit the grind, keep the farm.

For busy diversified growers
Why we're doing this

Farming is beautiful.

But life is richer when you still have energy for the people in it.

The problem
Labor grows faster than sales
The work multiplies as the farm scales. Revenue doesn't keep up. Most growers end up working 80hr/week just to stay afloat.

We've seen it too many times: farmers who genuinely love what they do, slowly worn down by the pressure that comes with it. The long days, the stress that never really switches off, the feeling that the farm always needs more than you have to give.

Vegetables sell cheap. Margins are thin. To make ends meet, most growers subsidize their farm with the only thing that's free: their own labor.

They make it work with very little, but that culture of "just grind harder" makes the problem invisible until burnout hits.

The program

We need to cut labor without cutting sales.

Most growers know something needs to change. The problem isn't motivation or effort. It's that the knowledge to make it happen has never been easy to find. It lives in the heads of a handful of experts, scattered across research papers and the hard-won experience of farmers who figured it out the slow way.

By putting our strengths together, farm experience, greenhouse knowledge, and a big community of farming brains, we believe we can crack the nut of building a profitable farm that runs on 40 hours a week.

We build the playbook Between us, we've had the chance to learn from hundreds of market gardeners, experts, researchers and our own farms. Now, we're getting what we learned out of our heads. Make it useful. Concrete. Actionable.
You bring the real-world problems Every farm is different. When you follow the playbook and hit an obstacle we didn't anticipate, we'll work through it with you. We'll then make the playbook stronger so everyone benefits.
The playbook gets sharper every week Transforming a farm to run on 40hr/week is a hard problem. We don't have all the answers yet. But we believe we'll get there, and building the tools alongside real farmers is the only way to do it.
The playbook

The greenhouse multiplier effect

We start with the greenhouse. The technical challenges are real, but the potential is clear enough that this is where we're confident the playbook needs to start.

1
Your most valuable crop is also your most time-consuming
Most field beds bring in low hundreds of dollars. A well-run greenhouse bed can bring in thousands. And greenhouse crops are already where you spend most of your time. Improving a radish crop won't move the needle. Improving your tomatoes changes everything.
The biggest labor savings are hiding in the crops with the biggest upside.
2
You can easily automate the work
Rolling up the sides at sunrise. Irrigating many times per day. Running back to protect crops from the cold. Always remembering to remember. Every day. Yet, in a greenhouse, you can automate a lot of the work. You'll get better results without having to think about it.
Turn the 24/7 worries into reliable harvests and cut on labor without losing a single sale.
3
Most growers never tap the real potential
Unlocking the greenhouse's potential is highly technical. Without the right expertise farmers end up needing far more beds just to hit the same harvest targets.
Same sales. A lot less labor.
The plan

Building the farm engine

The virtuous cycle: each loop cuts more field labor without touching your revenue. Every farmer who runs the cycle feeds back what they learn.

1
Increase yields
Plant biology doesn't limit us. Better techniques mean a bigger harvest on the same bed.
2
Grow fewer beds
Double your yields, cut half the beds. Same sales, but you saved a morning per week for every bed you cut.
3
Move more crops into the greenhouse
Each crop moved in lets you cut 2 more field beds, and feeds right back into step 1.
Repeat → each bed cut removes walking, weeding, troubleshooting. The hidden labor that eats your week. Without touching your revenue.

How it works

It's about not doing it alone for once.

The loop works on paper. But farms are messy, seasons are unpredictable, and motivation fades. That's where the community comes in.

It's easy to get lost. It's easy to get stuck. It's easy to lose motivation. We believe the momentum of a focused community can fix that.
